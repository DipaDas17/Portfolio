Research on Fishermen in Sunamgonj District

I conducted a qualitative case study in Derai Upazila, using in-depth interviews to enhance the validity and reliability of the research.

In Sunamgonj District, Bangladesh, many traditional fishermen, who have practiced fishing for generations, are being forced to shift to other occupations. These sole fishermen—whose ancestors migrated to the Haor region for fishing—have long depended on these waters for their livelihood. However, with the rise of Community-Based Fisheries Management (CBFM), their inherited fishing rights have been taken away. This shift has had severe socio-economic consequences. Many fishermen, lacking alternative skills or resources, face increased marginalization, economic instability, and social displacement. Their capabilities and traditional knowledge, once essential for sustainable fishing, are now undervalued, leaving them struggling for survival in an uncertain future.
